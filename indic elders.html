Elders CALLIGRAPHY

Indic calligraphy refers to the traditional art of writing in India and other South Asian countries. It involves the skilled use of a writing instrument, such as a pen or brush, to create beautiful and ornate letters and characters. Indic calligraphy is known for its flowing curves, intricate details, and decorative flourishes.

Here are many scripts used in the Indian subcontinent, so choose the script that you would like to learn. Devanagari is one of the most commonly used scripts and is used to write Hindi, Marathi, and Sanskrit, among others. Bengali is used to write Bengali, Gujarati for Gujarati, and so on. Calligraphy requires specific tools, such as a calligraphy pen or brush and ink. You can find these at art supply stores or online. You may also want to invest in calligraphy paper or practice pads to help you get started.
